"Don't be left behind - You can be the first to have the latest and greatest e-Grips sets, fresh out of the ovens! See details below the current First Dibs"

November 10': Zen SP Slopers

There are varying degrees of depth and diameter in the raked line carvings, but each is a little sculpted masterpiece. These low profile sand piles will build up the calluses on your tips, centimeter by centimeter.
- 10 holds per set.

December 10': Remarkable Rocks Mini-Jugs

One of the most popular new themes, the Remarkable Rocks Jugs, now have a little brother set. The Mini-Jugs are still very positive shapes, but have a small footprint – great incut grabs for walls with limited space options.
- 5 L holds in a set.

January 11': Quarry Pinches

The year of setter's dreams is 2010, with these open hand slopers added alongside the amazing O'Myorcan Ripple and Myorcan TufaPinch. The finite ripples along the surface of the grabs make hitting and pulling off them a question of precision and core strength. Add the classic Myorcan Tufas and Mini Water Tufas to complete your collection.
- 5 holds per set.

February 11': 2Tex Bubble Wrap Crimps

Our friend Kynan Waggoner had long had the idea for applying the 2Tex to Bubble Wrap shapes and it was first specifically introduced in his Signature set 2Tex Bubble Wrap Edges. These ten shapes are more intentionally competition grips - directional, low profile, hard-to-hold crimps.
- 10 medium holds in a set.

March 11': Median Loaves

These long and narrow pinch grips with the simple pure line aesthetic complement the original wider hand size Loaves and the more modest Demi Loaves.
-5 large holds in a set.

April 11': Peabody Flake Jugs

A rarity in the world of climbing holds, protruding flake jugs that are still very friendly to grab.
- 5 large holds in a set.

May11': Horse Pens Moderates

Sculpted with subtlety, these intricate slopers make for great technical routes on slight overhangs, or work equally well for hard dynamic and powerful sequences on steeper walls. Pair them up with the already classic Horse Pens Giantsto get the full southern sandstone feel. 5 Large Holds in a set.

June 11': Real Hueco Directionals

Any routesetter knows how important it can be to have "directional" shapes, best usable pulling in one orientation, and leaving few options for feet from above - these are likely to become classics in real-rock designed shapes. Set them up on a slight overhang with the Real Hueco Pinch Jugs or Real Hueco Incuts.
- 5 large holds in a set.

July 11': Myorcan Sloper Edges

The Ty Foose limestone ripple designs represented in the original Myorcan Tufas and in 2010 brought to the Myorcan Slopers have been copied by many but cannot truly be replicated. These ten shapes continue the brilliant theme.
- 10 medium holds per set.

September 11': Mo Joe's Slopers

Lying low for years in the hallowed ground of CATS, were a couple simple yet elegant sandstone slopers from an earlier generation of shaping. Chris Danielson introduces his first set here, with a set based on those classics.
- 5 large holds in a set.

October 11': Caterpillar Finger Buckets

From the first set, Caterpillar Edges, a new theme was created. These positive finger bucket shapes continue the caterpillar style with friendly two-pad protruding grabs from a smooth and simple base.
- 5 medium holds in a set.

November 11': Remarkable Rocks Pinches

Ryan Spence delivers - shaping one of the most versatile sets in the e-Grips line. Each of the five intricately sculpted shapes will seem like a completely new grip when rotated 90 degrees.
- 5 L holds in a set.
